CPSC547 2022WT1: UBC CS Grads Cost of Living

Welcome!

In May 2022, some UBC Computer Science (CS) graduate students distributed surveys to the other students in the department to determine how well students were being supported by their current stipends. This explainer uses the survey data, and data from government and university websites to demonstrate the current cost of living situation in Vancouver (as of Dec 2022), see how students are being supported, and compare your own budget to that of an average CS graduate student. Scroll through the article for more information!

So what expenses does a UBC CS grad student have?

Focusing on the basic expenses of a CS graduate student, this shows the average proportion of their expenses separated into housing, food, social activities, health, utilies, and commuting expenditures.

How can we calculate inflation in Vancouver?

The consumer price index (CPI) is a common econcomic metric used to measure the inflation rate in a certain economic market (such as Vancouver). It is a proportional metric used to compare prices of an item now to the price of the same item some years ago. For example, if a banana cost $2 in 2022, but now costs $4 in 2022. The CPI for the banana would be: 200. Note, there are not units for this metric.

Inflation Rate at British Columbia

Here we have the overaall CPI for British Columbia since 2016. The CPI calculated here is based on comparing prices of items to their original price in 2002. The visualization here depicts the overal upward trend of CPI indicating that inflation has been increasing since 2002.

The 12-month % change

An overall change of inflation over the course of 2 decades is not surprising. But let's compare the CPI change from year to year. In this visualization, we have how CPI has changed compared to the previous year. If you look at 2022, this rate is strikingly fast compared to previous years.

Components

Let's look at the previous year a little most closely. In this graph every line represents the change in CPI from October 2021 to September 2022. We can calculate CPI for different components, such as food and hosuing. Looks like in August of 2022, clothing was overall less expensive than in August of 2021.

Housing!

But housing has one of the highest 12-month CPI over the last year. A CPI of 8.26% means that if your rent was 1600$ last year, it will be 1732$ this year. This is particularly concerning for graduate students since housing is the largest monthly expense that students have.

OK. Well what about CS grad student funding?

Each shape you see here depicts one student who filled out the cost of living survey. And each shape represents if that student is in a masters, Ph.D. or Ph.D.-track program. In this visualization, we are looking at how a students basic income (their research assitantship (RA-ship) + teaching assistantship (TA-ship)) compares to the number of expenses they have.

Oh no!

From our survey, the funding of 47.5% graduate students in UBC CS department cannot offset their basic expenses! Each student highlighted in red has more expenses than they do income.

With additional support...

Some students receieve additional financial support in terms of awards or internships. All students that receive this extra support can afford all their basic expenses and perhaps even save a little on the side.

However, there are students are who still cannot overcome their expenses because they currently have no extra support available.

There are also those with "just enough" funding, but they likely are unable to save as they just meet their basic needs.

You can use the filter button to see how this data is spread amongst degree program.

An unsafe feeling.

88.6% of the respondents to the cost of living survey said they are feeling unsafe in current grad stipend amount.

Does the funding offset the expenses?

Here we have overlaid the cost of living on top of the income that the Ph.D. students receive. In some universities, like MIT, we see that students actually earn more than they spend. But in other universities, like UBC and McGill, the expenses that students have is greater than the income they receive. Boom! After adding the cost of living, the problem arises!

Ordering the comparison...

After ordering the comparison we see that students at UBC have the greatest deficit when it comes to their income vs their expenses compared to other universities.
